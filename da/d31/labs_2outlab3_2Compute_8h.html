Computes the Gauss-Legendre node for a given index and polynomial degree.

The roots of the Legendre polynomial of degree n are symmetric around the origin and are located in the interval [-1, 1]. They are also close to the zeros of the Chebyshev polynomials of the first kind, which are given by:

x_i = cos((2i - 1) * π / (2n))

However, the roots of the Legendre polynomial are slightly shifted towards the center compared to the zeros of the Chebyshev polynomial. To account for this shift, we can use the following formula as the initial guess for the Newton-Raphson method:

x_i = cos(π * (i - 0.25) / (n + 0.5))

This function will fill with Chebyshev polynomial guesses and iterate towards finding the roots using the Newton-Raphson method.

Template Parameters
TThe numeric type of the Gauss-Legendre node.
Parameters
indexThe index of the Gauss-Legendre node.
nThe degree of the Legendre polynomial.
Returns
The Gauss-Legendre node for the given index and polynomial degree.
